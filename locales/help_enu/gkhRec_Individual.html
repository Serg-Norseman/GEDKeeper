Person Record

The Person record is the foundation of a genealogical database. Such a record contains all information about an individual. GEDKeeper allows you to store full name, nickname, and sex in a person record. You can also attach all events and facts that relate to the person's life. Using GEDKeeper you easily investigate family and social relations of the person (associations with other people). GEDKeeper does not restrict the number of persons or families. You can also divide people by different social groups, add additional indicators to improve obviousness (references and footnotes), add unlimited number of notes, multimedia materials, and sources. You may also add a person's portrait to be displayed in trees.

The following are categories that may be edited:

  • Event represents an event or fact from a person's life.
  • Family lists marriages of a person or family relations. Children common to both are specified in a dedicated dialog.
  • Association lists a person's social relations.
  • Group is a way to unite persons by categories. This makes navigation and search more effective. For instance, a group of "WWI Veterans" may be used.
  • Note is any arbitrary text about person.
  • Multimedia content combines photos, scanned documents, and portraits.
  • Source represents documents used, correspondence, and any other kind of source of information about a person.
  • Reference/footnote is an additional indicator for GEDKeeper itself for a way to create handy classifications.

Changing person information

  • You change the parents of a person by using the special dedicated panel with two fields—for person's father and mother, and three groups of buttons. At first glance this may look complex, but in actuality this provides flexible editing of object properties. Button group , , and to the right of the parent names modifies parents' family as a whole object. That is, you can use button to attach affected person to an existing family. You use the button to modify properties of an already attached family without the need to close the currently opened person editing window. You use to the right of the parent names to detach a person from the family.
  • The same buttons: , , and located under the name of each parent perform other functions. The button adds a family if one was not added already, and then adds the concrete parent to this family (father or mother, depending on the selected section). The button removes a parent (father or mother) from a person's family. To quickly navigate to a parent's person record you use the button.
  • There is a dedicated edit box to the right of a person's full name and sex—where you can enter additional information about person name including nickname, pseudonym, status, and name prefix and suffix.
  • To modify an object of any type, that is related to the affected person, you use a specific tab and the following standard buttons: , , and . You can add any number of events to the affected person, that are related to his/her life, various attributes, notes, photos or other multimedia content, source materials mentioning the person, marriages, and groups. You use the special list of associations to add additional relations between people.
  • You change information about a married couple on the "Spouses" tab using the , , and buttons. When you add or change one of conjoints a family editor window appears. This window allows you to add a spouse, children, or an event. Using arrow buttons you can change the order of marriages for the selected person.

There is a uniting table on the "Persons" tab of the main working window that includes all persons available in the database. A Person summary to the right of the table shows all information about a selected person and references other records.
