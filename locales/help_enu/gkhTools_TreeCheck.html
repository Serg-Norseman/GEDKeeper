Tools: Database Check

This tool checks information correctness in a database. It checks two types of records: family and person. Unlike the usual database integrity checking, which runs every time a database is opened to check the database itself, this tool handles logical errors and data inaccuracy.

To pass the check, a family record must have an assigned husband, wife, children, and events. When an empty family is found, the tool proposes to remove it. It is normal practice to create a family from people, which as it will be discovered later, are from different families and maybe added erroneously. In these cases, GEDKeeper can easily move a person from one family to another. After such actions a family record may lose all relations. This is a very likely situation when you actively process large amounts of information.

A person record is checked as follows:

  • If the death event is checked, you are notified if the date is outside the maximal, scientifically confirmed age limit.
  • Notifies about people without an explicitly defined sex.
  • Notifies about incorrect lifetime dates (for example, death date is before birth date).
  • Notifies about persons that were married before or after reasonable limits.
  • Notifies about children that are not born within reasonable limits.
When a person has no identified sex, the record is marked as being changed and opens the sex editor dialog. When a person's age exceeds the maximum, the tool will add a death event, without a specified date, to the person record.

GEDKeeper fixes the lack of a death event and sex only. Under some conditions empty family records are removed. Any other issues are displayed for your information only.
