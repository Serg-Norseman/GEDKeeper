Tools: Search for Patriarchs

This patriarchy tool searches the most remote ancestor in each investigated family tree. GEDKeeper searches through different families in the database and selects a male from each family that has no parents and has a specified number of descendant generations. When an ancestor has an unspecified year of birth, this year is evaluated automatically—based on the year of birth of the closest descendants. Such rough calculations are made for evaluation only. For each patriarch GEDKeeper calculates both number of generations and number of descendants.

Before you begin to work with this search, you must define the length of a target family branch. For example, if you need all patriarchs that have at least three descendant generations, enter this number in the "Generations of not less than" field. Then press the "Search" button. The search will find all matching patriarchs.

The "Ignore dates" option forces GEDKeeper to not calculate the birthday of a patriarch. In this case, a patriarch without a defined birthday will show "-1" as the year of birth.

A person with a "patriarch label" has the [*] sign before its name (see Label for an individual record).

You can use the "Assign the sign" feature to set a "patriarch label" without going to a person editor window.

The "Diagram" button displays a "Graph of interconnections between patriarchs" diagram. This is a specialized tool that may appear complicated at first glance. To reduce the number of vertices in the graph, we recommend setting a large enough number of patriarch's descendant generations before viewing the diagram. After the diagram has been built and stabilized, you can see patriarchs on the the diagram as dark-blue vertices and intersections of their descendants' branches as lilac vertices. A digital signature on a node is a family identifier. Hovering the mouse over a vertex shows the name of the family in this node. Arrows are directed from ancestor's family to descendant's one—where two kins intersect.
