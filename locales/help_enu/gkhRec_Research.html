Research Record

You use research records to manage researching main directions and prioritize those directions. You can create a research record for a family branch and assign a relative priority for this research. Then you track a period of time when the research is in progress, check the current research status, and the completion state.

You can split research up into individual tasks and add correspondence with other persons (relatives, researchers, etc.) using a correspondence record. Add social groups to easily find all people that were researched under this search direction.

You add a description of search progress and required comments using text notes.

There is a uniting table on the "Researches" tab of the main working window which includes all researches available in the database. A research summary to the right of the table shows all information about the selected research.

See also: Task, Correspondence, Group, Note.
